Google’s tools are everywhere. For years, Google Forms has been the default option for surveys, registrations, and feedback collection. But convenience comes at a cost: data privacy, ecosystem lock-in, and limited control.

## The Problem with Google Forms
Google Forms is free, but “free” isn’t the whole story. Here’s what users often overlook:

- **Data ownership:** Responses are stored in Google’s infrastructure, not yours.  
- **Privacy concerns:** Google’s business model revolves around data. Even if Forms isn’t directly monetized, it feeds into the larger ecosystem.  
- **Limited customization:** Branding, design, and advanced features are restricted.  
- **Account dependency:** Many features require a Google account, which ties respondents into Google’s ecosystem.  
- **Vendor lock-in:** If you want to move away from Google, Forms becomes a weak link.  

For individuals, small businesses, nonprofits, and privacy-conscious organizations, these trade-offs are increasingly unacceptable.

## How to Migrate from Google Forms to Fabform.io
Switching is easier than you think. Here’s a step-by-step guide:

1. **Audit your existing forms**  
   List the forms you currently use in Google Forms (surveys, registrations, feedback).  

2. **Recreate them in Fabform.io**  
   Use Fabform.io’s builder to replicate your questions, logic, and design.  

3. **Customize branding**  
   Add your logo, colors, and styling to make forms look professional.  

4. **Embed or share**  
   Replace old Google Form links with Fabform.io links or embed codes.  

5. **Redirect responses**  
   Export old Google Form responses if needed, and start collecting new ones in Fabform.io.  

6. **Communicate the change**  
   Let your audience know you’ve switched to a privacy-friendly alternative.
